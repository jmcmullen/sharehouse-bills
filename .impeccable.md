# Impeccable Design Guidelines

## Design Context

### Users

A single sharehouse — the owner/admin (Jay) plus a small group of trusted housemates. They use the app to:

- Check what they owe and what's been paid, usually on a phone
- Upload/receive bill PDFs and watch them get split automatically
- Confirm Up Bank transfers have landed against the right debt
- Manage recurring charges (rent, utilities) without thinking about it

Context of use is domestic and low-frequency: a glance after getting paid, a quick check when a bill arrives. Not a tool they live in. Mobile-first matters; desktop is the admin surface.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: playful, friendly, casual.**

Voice should feel like a housemate who handles the admin well — not a finance app lecturing you about balances. Money is the subject, but the tone is "sorted, no stress" rather than "serious, important."

Emotional goals:
- **Reassurance** — you can trust the numbers, the splits are fair
- **Lightness** — owing rent is already annoying; the UI shouldn't compound it
- **Momentum** — paying/settling should feel satisfying, not bureaucratic

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** warm, human, modern-fintech. The existing palette (warm amber/tan primary in oklch, soft off-white backgrounds, warm neutrals) is the anchor — keep leaning into that warmth rather than drifting toward cool grays.

**References (draw from):**
- **Up Bank / Revolut** — friendly modern fintech. Rounded forms, confident color use, expressive typography, data that feels alive rather than tabular. Micro-interactions that acknowledge user action. Up especially: the way it turns transactions into a human timeline.

**Anti-references (avoid):**
- **Corporate banking / enterprise** — no dense spreadsheet layouts, no sterile gray-on-gray, no "Quarterly Reconciliation Report" energy
- **Generic AI/SaaS template** — no purple/indigo gradients, no stock dashboard clichés, no "Acme Inc" shadcn-out-of-the-box look
- **Overly cutesy / childish** — no emoji overload, no comic-book bubbles, no pastel-chaos. Playful ≠ juvenile
- **Brutalist / raw** — no harsh black borders, no all-monospace, no aggressive asymmetry

**Theme:** both light and dark, system-default via `next-themes`. Both modes get equal polish — dark is not an afterthought.

**Accessibility:** solid defaults. WCAG AA contrast, full keyboard navigation, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations. No formal audit required, but don't ship something that would fail one.

### Design Principles

1. **Warm over clinical.** Lean into the amber palette. When in doubt between a neutral gray and a warmer tone, pick the warmer tone. Money apps usually feel cold — this one shouldn't.

2. **Mobile is the default surface.** Design phone-first, then scale up. Touch targets ≥44px, thumb-reachable primary actions, dense desktop tables collapse to human-readable cards on mobile.

3. **Playful in motion, calm at rest.** The static UI should feel composed and confident. Personality comes through in transitions, empty states, success moments, and copy — not in loud static elements.

4. **Numbers are the hero.** Amounts owed, amounts paid, splits — typography should make these legible at a glance. Tabular numerals, clear hierarchy, never bury the number that matters most.

5. **Respect the household's intimacy.** This isn't a product for strangers — it's a tool for people who share a kitchen. Copy can be direct and slightly casual ("Jay owes $240", not "Outstanding balance: Mr. McMullen, AUD 240.00"). Avoid corporate hedging.
